Typography Fundamentals

Understanding the building blocks of great typography

What is Typography?

Typography is the visual component of the written word. It involves:

  • Typeface selection: Choosing appropriate fonts
  • Hierarchy: Creating visual order and importance
  • Spacing: Managing white space and letter spacing
  • Readability: Ensuring text is easy to read
  • Mood & tone: Conveying emotion through type

Good typography enhances communication and creates better user experiences.

Key Typography Terms

Typeface vs Font

Typeface is the design (Helvetica), Font is the file (Helvetica Bold 16px)

Kerning

Adjusting space between individual letter pairs

Leading

Vertical space between lines of text (line-height in CSS)

Tracking

Overall spacing between all letters in a word or line

Font Pairing Examples

Playfair Display

Paired with Inter for a classic yet modern combination. The serif headline creates elegance while the sans-serif body ensures readability.

Poppins

Combined with Merriweather creates a friendly, approachable feel. The geometric sans-serif headlines with serif body text work beautifully together.

Pairing Tip: Combine fonts with contrasting characteristics but similar proportions. Avoid pairing fonts that are too similar or compete for attention.

Optimal Line Length

Too Narrow (35 characters)

This line length is too narrow and creates too many line breaks, making reading choppy and inefficient.

Optimal (45-75 characters)

This line length is optimal for reading comfort. It allows the eye to scan efficiently without losing track of the next line. Most typography experts recommend 45-75 characters per line.

Too Wide (100+ characters)

This line length is too wide and makes it difficult for readers to track from the end of one line to the beginning of the next. Long lines tire the eye and reduce reading comprehension, especially on larger screens where text can stretch across the entire viewport.

Typography in Action

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. The arrangement of type involves selecting typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing, and adjusting the space between pairs of letters.

Good typography enhances the reading experience and helps communicate your message effectively. It creates hierarchy, establishes mood, and guides the reader's eye through your content.

Key Principles

  • Readability comes first
  • Establish clear hierarchy
  • Use appropriate spacing
  • Consider your audience
